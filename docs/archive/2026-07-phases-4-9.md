## Phase 10 — close the A2 grammar standard

A2 had been called complete from its lexical coverage while structural coverage was only 20/30.
Phase 10 introduced the grammar inventory, coverage computation, validator and regression
tripwire, then closed all ten missing points. The final measured result is 30/30.

- **P10-1:** grammar manifest, report, validator wiring and tripwire test.
- **P10-2:** *Infinitiv mit zu* and *um … zu*, placed after the subordinate-clause foundation.
- **P10-3:** nominative and accusative relative clauses as A2 syntax.
- **P10-4:** conjunctional adverbs with inversion and bounded-past *als* vs repeated *wenn*.
- **P10-5:** Futur I and dative reflexive extensions in existing compatible topics.
- **P10-6:** indefinite `man` plus productive `von + Dativ` and receptive common-noun genitive.
- **P10-7:** present passive at the standard's receptive A2 scope.
- **P10-8:** computed publication of the structural figure and correction of the B1 blueprint so
  B1 deepens rather than re-teaches the A2 half.

The number is explicitly structural: it proves that every manifest point has teaching evidence,
not that every explanation is linguistically signed off or that a learner has mastered it. The
separate 30-point linguistic pass and its corrections are recorded in
[`../a2-linguistic-qa.md`](../a2-linguistic-qa.md).

## Phase 4 — complete A2

### P4-1 · Freeze the A2 curriculum contract — `done` (S)

Rewrite the blueprint against the post-hardening authoring contract, and freeze the identities: the
spine order, the topic ids, the outcome ids, the focus-tag additions and the atlas groups. All of
them become persisted keys in the learner's progress the moment a unit ships.

Two decisions carry the weight. **Nebensätze move from last to sixth** — four of the units that
would have preceded them cannot decline an invitation or explain an absence without *weil*, and
declaring those items `preview: true` across three units would be a way of admitting the order is
wrong without fixing it. **An A2 unit owns a thin new A2 deck and never adopts an A1 one** —
adoption would flip that deck's fresh-card gate from "any A1 topic opened" to "this A2 topic
opened", pushing hundreds of A1 Wortliste words behind an A2 gate.

Closed with bundle 2, which is when the last two disagreements surfaced — both of them in the
direction the acceptance criterion warns about, a document describing a system that does not exist.
The blueprint gave `gesundheit-arzttermin` a `gesundheit-koerper` group of its own under
`wortschatz`; the node is `communication`, the group would have been `vocabulary`, and the rule one
line above the table forbids the mismatch. The group had been added to `content/atlas.yaml` and was
held by no node. And `arbeit-beruf`'s `deepens` had grown a third edge (`termine-vereinbaren`, which
is real — the unit drills `du-sie`) that the blueprint's table never gained.

### P4-2 · Build the A2 instrument — `done` (L)

The measurement A2 authoring will be judged by, built before the content it judges.

- **A validator rule: every declared outcome must be measured.** Four A2 outcomes
  (`dativ-pronomen`, `dativ-praepositionen`, `dativ-verben`, `modal-konjugation`) were declared in
  `content/atlas.yaml` and referenced by no exercise item and no reading question. They could never
  light up, and a probe on them could never arm. Fixed the four, then made it impossible to ship a
  fifth.
- Six A2 probe families with parallel variants, following the A1 pattern, arming the A2 retention
  clock immediately.
- ~~`deepens` semantics in training~~ — **already true; do not build it.** `focusStats`
  (`src/lib/weakness.ts`) keys weakness by tag alone and is blind to the topic an attempt came
  from, so an error while practising a deepening topic marks that confusion weak course-wide;
  `buildSession` draws its second band from the entire eligible pool, and the base topic hands over
  its items carrying that tag. Measured on the shipped content: four errors on
  `dativ-praepositionen` inside `dativ` resurface `stadt-wege`'s items on that tag in 200 sessions
  out of 200, and nothing else from `stadt-wege` enters. **A `deepens`-aware aggregation would be a
  regression**: scoping a tag to a topic narrows a signal that is deliberately global. What was
  genuinely missing is now shipped: `bun run validate` rejects a `deepens` edge whose two ends
  share no tag the base can be drilled on (`tests/deepens.test.ts`).
- A Goethe-A2 Wortliste manifest (`data/goethe-a2-wortliste.txt`) and `goetheCoverage(level)`
  generalized from `goetheA1Coverage()`.
- A checkpoint route not hard-wired to A1 (`a1/checkpoint-a1` was named in five places).

### P4-4 · Author the ten A2 units — `done` (L)

Shipped in three bundles, each unit one complete bundle (atlas node + unit slot, article, pretest,
practice sets clearing the item-mix bar, probe family, intensive reading, thin deduped A2 deck,
registered focus tags). A learner-led seventeenth A2 unit, `verben-mit-praepositionen`, was later
inserted after `gesundheit-arzttermin`, preserving all identities.

What bundle 3's review round found was an **instrument** defect live since A1:

> A `translate` item that grades word order could not attribute its own signature error.

`diffExpectedWords` is an LCS alignment, and a transposition is not a missing word: `… weil ich am
Samstag **arbeiten muss**` typed as `… muss arbeiten` still contains both words, so the diff
flagged only `arbeiten` and reported no graded token as diverged — logged wrong but
**unattributed**. Every verb-final family was affected (`nebensatz-verbende`, `indirekte-frage`,
`modal-satzklammer`, `trennbar-modal`, `perfekt-satzklammer`): 34 items, 12 of them probe variants,
so retention curves for word-order rules were read off items that could not measure them. CLAUDE.md
had told every author the opposite ("tokens are compared positionally"), which is the sentence that
produced all 34. Fixed in the scorer: `gradeTranslation` also asks whether the learner put
something *else* into a slot the item grades, against the accepted rendering they came closest to.
The same round fixed dictation attribution (31 items), where a mistyped noun was logged as the
grammar confusion the `listen` item drills.

### P4-6 · Give the writing outcomes verified evidence — `done` (S)

Five A2 outcomes were measured *only* by a `write` item — legal, honest, and permanently dark,
since `write` logs unverified evidence. Done in PR #38 on the `profil-schreiben` model: each now
carries a verified `translate` alongside its `write`. **All 61 A2 outcomes have a verified
measurement.** One correction: `nachbarn-bitte` was carried by a `speak`, not a `write` — same
defect. Both new items use a focus their own topic **owns** rather than `preview: true`: tagging
`hoeflich-konjunktiv` in unit 7 would have marked the learner weak on a confusion the course does

### P4-5 · Close A2 — `done` (M)

The cumulative A2 checkpoint and page, the Goethe-A2 Wortliste completion pass, A2 extensive
readers, computed Über claims, the 0.3.0 release. Done in PRs #30–#38. **A2: 1449/1449 (100%).
A1: 673/673.**

**Why P3-6 no longer blocked this:** the gate's purpose was to stop authoring if A1 missed the bar,
and all sixteen A2 units were already written — so it is what it always really was, a **revision
trigger** (cohort read 2026-08-02; a miss drives a 0.3.x revision of the lesson pattern). A release
is a distribution event, not a claim about retention.

**What the work turned out to be about:** the *instrument* could not be trusted with the claim it
was about to make. A `~` in a manifest counted toward the published percentage on the manifest's
own say-so, and nine marks were false — eight words appearing nowhere in `content/`, and A1's
`euer`, which appeared only inside English prose *about* German. `bun run validate` now hard-fails
an unearned `~`, and the check went on to refuse its author's own frozen plan (teaching
`Disko`/`Klub` inside vocab example sentences — which `taughtSurface` excludes, correctly).

## Phase 6 — learning quality: finish A2 on honest signal

Instrument first, then content. The 2026-07-14 audit showed thirty-two rejected production
renderings awaiting a linguistic ruling with 35 attempts withheld from the focus signals behind
them (the queue's silent top-ten display cap, removed by P6-1, was hiding most), probe debt that
would compound once all seventeen A2 families armed, and the worst weak focus staying weak
*despite an existing drill*.

### P6-1 · Grading-decisions instrument — `done` 2026-07-14 (M)

The grading-review queue is derived from the attempt log and has no memory: without a committed
home for rulings it could never drain. `data/grading-decisions.yaml` with a Zod schema; entries
`{item, given, decision: accept|constrain|confirm, note, decidedAt}`, `given` matched via
`normalizeTranslation`. **accept** — correct, target-preserving German: the same PR adds it to the
item's `accept` list and bumps `revision`; withheld attempts stay excluded. **constrain** — good
German that bypasses the target: bilingual `instruction` constraint + bump; attempts stay excluded.
**confirm** — the rejection was right: the exclusion lifts and the attempts re-enter `focusSignals`
with attribution **recomputed under today's grading contract**, never the stored historical tag — a
stale attribution from an older grader re-entering the weak-focus table is exactly the false entry
the weakness signal must not contain. Audit reports undecided-only; validator fails a dangling item
ref and an accept-decided rendering today's grader still rejects; orphans warn.

### P6-2 · Triage the queued renderings — `done` 2026-07-14 (M)

All thirty-two, using `--item` evidence: each rendering got a linguistic ruling and — for
accept/constrain — its paired content edit and `revision` bump in the same change. Post-merge the
audit was rerun and the weak-focus table re-read before any drill authoring.

### P6-3 · Probe catch-up pacing — `done` 2026-07-15 (M)

When due probes exceed `MAX_PROBES_PER_SESSION`, Heute shows a **"Probe-Rückstand"** card opening a
probes-only run (up to five, most-overdue first, nothing else in the visit — measurement-clean by
construction); ordinary sessions keep the cap at three. Chosen over raising the cap: nine probes
before practice turns a session into an exam, and fatigue confounds the measurement. One new audit
row (probe debt + actually-elapsed interval distribution) with named consumers (P5-11 audits, the
P5-7 decision). Overdue probes are not invalid data — the report gives the interval that actually
elapsed.

### P6-4 · Dative-cluster drill — `done` 2026-07-15 (M)

`content/exercises/a2/drill-dativ-ausloeser.yaml` (`role: drill` on `dativ`), authored against the

### P6-7 · Minimal-ceremony open production — `done` 2026-07-15 (M)

The P6-6 verification found the speak item technically working and didactically hollow — and the
owner's ruling cut deeper than the widget: **the app cannot verify free speech or free writing**,
so the staged self-assessment flows — mandatory before/after checklists, forced second attempts —
were fake ceremony. Both open-production items are now single-pass. `Speak.tsx`: attempt → one
press → model beside the learner's own take; a stopped take auto-plays (autoplay refusal falls back
to a prominent listen button); re-recording stays available on the compare screen. `Write.tsx`:
draft → one press → model beside the learner's text, which stays editable — revision is an option,
not a stage — with the Schreib-Assistent attached right there. `checklist`/`requirements` render as
guidance text, never gated forms. Payloads slimmed to `{draft, revision}` / `{recorded}`;
`before`/`after` stay as optional legacy fields. Typical speak run: 4 presses, down from ~11.

### P6-8 · Goethe-B1 Wortliste manifest — `done` 2026-07-15 (M)

`data/goethe-b1-wortliste.txt`: **3,416 headwords** transcribed headwords-only from the official
PDF (which stays untracked — copyrighted), after the A2 normalization rules — m/f pairs split,
collapsed entries expanded, D/A/CH cross-references each contributing their word. Verified by two

### P8-4 · Content-language machinery: `uk` and `de` — `done` 2026-07-15 (L)

Ships **both** new explanation halves. Optional `uk` on `bilingualSchema` plus the parallel
optionals (`title_uk`; vocab `uk`/`example_uk`; `prompt_uk`; outcome `uk`), **and** an optional
`de` half — German-medium explanations, authored for B1 from day one and never backfilled to A1/A2
(a hide-the-EN/RU-prose immersion mode was considered and rejected). Validator letter-set checks:
і/ї/є/ґ in `ru` fails, ы/э/ъ/ё in `uk` fails, Cyrillic in `de` fails. Parity per-file, per-node for
`content/atlas.yaml`. Glosses grow to `[[de::en::ru::uk]]`, all-or-none per reading.
`Uk.astro`/`De.astro`; `ExplainLang` becomes `en | ru | uk | de`; `pick()` falls back `uk→en` and
`de→en`. The CSS fallback is pure `:has()` — the EN half hides only where a same-parent `uk`/`de`

contract commented in `global.css`). Parity satisfiability forced `title_uk` onto every
`title_ru`-bearing schema (zod strips unknown keys). `content/reference-data` is exempt from `de`
parity (its `{de,en,ru}` records are German example sentences). Two traps fixed in the same change:
a reading gloss's `de` field is the glossed phrase itself, so `ReadingText` destructures to
`{en, ru, uk}` before `pick()`; and widening `ExplainLang` flushed out the last three hand-narrowed
`'en' | 'ru'` types. No `prompt_de` (a translate prompt exists to be translated *into* German) and
no vocab `de` gloss (a card's meaning side is never German).
