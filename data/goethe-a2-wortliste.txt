# Goethe-Zertifikat A2 — Wortliste coverage manifest.
# Headwords only (the official glosses and examples are copyrighted; all
# translations and examples in content/vocab/ are original). One headword per
# line, bare form matching the vocab `de` field. The first sections mirror the
# PDF's own Wortgruppen (pp. 5-7); the rest re-groups the alphabetical list
# (pp. 8-32) into themes we can build decks around. A word listed in several
# sections counts once, for its first.
#
# A leading ~ marks words addressed by grammar topics (prepositions, determiners,
# conjunctions, W-words, sein) - the curriculum teaches them, no flashcard needed.
# THE ~ IS CHECKED, NOT TRUSTED: `bun run validate` fails unless the word actually
# occurs in a topic article (outside the En/Ru explanation blocks), a reading, or
# a practice/drill item. It used to be a self-certification, and eight words were
# marked here that appeared nowhere in content/ at all. A ~ you cannot pay for
# with content is a word that needs a flashcard instead - which is why `wegen` and
# `außerhalb` are now card words: they carry a translatable meaning ("because of",
# "outside of") and teaching them as case grammar would drag the Genitiv into A2.
#
# Extracted from the official PDF (Goethe-Institut, 2016, "circa 1300
# lexikalische Einheiten"); every headword below occurs verbatim in it.
# Notes on normalization:
#  - paired entries are split ("Arzt, ¨-e / Ärztin, -nen" -> Arzt + Ärztin);
#  - collapsed entries are expanded ("(ab)fahren" -> fahren + abfahren,
#    "Geburts- (jahr, ort, tag)" -> Geburtsjahr + Geburtsort + Geburtstag);
#  - determiner/adjective stems keep the PDF's form (all-, ander-, letzt-);
#  - the PDF's date/clock format demos and the symbol-only measures (1 cm,
#    2 km, 1 %, 1 l) carry no headword and are not listed.
